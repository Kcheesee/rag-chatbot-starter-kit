# Medication Reference — Important Disclaimer

**This is illustrative sample data for a software demo. It is NOT medical advice.**

This knowledge base exists to demonstrate a retrieval-augmented chatbot answering
from a high-stakes reference corpus with citations and guardrails. It is intentionally
limited to **general, educational reference information** about some common
over-the-counter (OTC) medications: the generic name, common brand names, the drug
class, and the general condition each is used for.

## What this reference does NOT do

- It does **not** provide dosing, frequency, or administration instructions.
- It does **not** diagnose conditions or recommend a specific medication for you.
- It does **not** cover interactions, contraindications, warnings, pregnancy/breastfeeding
  guidance, or side effects in any complete way.
- It is **not** a substitute for the product's official label ("Drug Facts" panel) or
  for a licensed clinician or pharmacist.

## Always do this instead

- **Read the product's Drug Facts label** and follow it.
- **Ask a pharmacist or doctor** before starting, stopping, or combining medications,
  especially if you are pregnant, nursing, have a medical condition, or take other drugs.
- **Call your local emergency number** for a suspected overdose or serious reaction, or
  contact a poison-control service.

If a question asks for a dose, a diagnosis, or "what should I take," the correct answer
is to decline and refer the person to a pharmacist or clinician — not to guess.
