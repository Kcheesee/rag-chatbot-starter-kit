# About This Knowledge Base

Illustrative sample data for a demo — verify with the official agency; not legal, tax, or financial advice.

## What This Is

This collection of documents is a small, illustrative **sample knowledge base** built to power a demonstration of a retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) chatbot. It plays the role of a friendly "citizen services" help desk that answers common questions about everyday interactions with United States public services — things like passports, federal income taxes, public benefits programs, and driver's licenses.

It exists so that developers, product teams, and evaluators can see how a heading-aware document splitter chunks content and how a retrieval pipeline surfaces relevant passages in response to natural-language questions.

## What This Is Not

This is **not** official guidance from any government agency. It is **not** a substitute for the authoritative information published by the relevant federal, state, or local authority. Nothing here is legal advice, tax advice, immigration advice, or financial advice.

The content has been written to be **general and timeless** on purpose. It deliberately avoids quoting specific dollar amounts, fees, filing deadlines, processing times, eligibility thresholds, or policy details that change from year to year or differ from place to place. Wherever a concrete current figure would matter, these documents tell you to check the official source instead of stating a number that could be wrong or out of date.

## How To Use It Responsibly

- Treat every answer derived from this corpus as a **starting point for understanding**, not as a final determination.
- Always **confirm the details with the official agency** that administers the program or service before you act.
- Requirements vary by **state, county, and individual circumstances**. Two people asking the same question may have genuinely different correct answers.
- When a document points you to "the official site" or "the relevant agency," follow that pointer. That is where the authoritative, current information lives.

## Scope Of The Corpus

The demo corpus covers a handful of widely encountered topics:

- **Passports** — applying for and renewing a U.S. passport book or card.
- **Federal income taxes** — the basics of who files, how, and where to get help.
- **Public benefits** — high-level concepts behind common assistance programs.
- **Identification and licenses** — driver's licenses, state IDs, and the REAL ID concept.

Each topic lives in its own Markdown file with a clear title and many sub-headings, which makes the content easy to chunk and retrieve.

## A Note On Tone

The writing aims to be **plain-language, neutral, and non-partisan**. It describes how services generally work in broad strokes, the way a calm, well-informed help-desk volunteer might, without taking political positions or making promises about outcomes.

## Disclaimer (Repeated For Clarity)

Illustrative sample data for a demo — verify with the official agency; not legal, tax, or financial advice. Always rely on the official, current source for anything that affects your money, your legal status, your benefits, or your travel.
