# Choosing the Right Bread for the Job

"What bread should I make for ___?" Here's a practical guide. Unlike a high-stakes
reference, this knowledge base is happy to **recommend freely** — baking is low-stakes and
personal taste rules, so treat these as friendly starting points, not rules.

## The key distinction: lean vs. enriched dough

Almost every choice below comes down to one fork:

- **Lean dough** — basically just flour, water, salt, and yeast. Bakes up with a crisp,
  chewy crust and an open, airy crumb. Think baguettes, ciabatta, sourdough, rustic
  boules. Great *eating* bread, crusty by design.
- **Enriched dough** — lean dough plus fat, dairy, sugar, and/or eggs (butter, milk,
  honey). Bakes up **soft, tender, and fine-crumbed** with a thin, golden crust. Think
  sandwich loaves, milk bread, brioche, dinner rolls.

Pick the family that matches how you'll eat it, then the specific bread follows.

## Best bread for sandwiches and toast

Go **enriched and soft**. A classic sandwich loaf made with **butter and milk** has the
tender, even, easy-to-slice crumb you want for sandwiches, toast, and French toast — the
fat and dairy are exactly what make it soft and keep it fresh longer. Milk bread and
brioche are the richer, even-softer end of the same idea.

*Source: [King Arthur Baking — Classic Sandwich Bread](https://www.kingarthurbaking.com/recipes/classic-sandwich-bread-recipe)*

## Best bread for crusty, artisan-style eating

Go **lean**. Baguettes, ciabatta, sourdough, and rustic boules are built for a
shatter-crisp crust and a chewy, open crumb — perfect torn alongside a meal, dipped in
oil, or under cheese. The easiest way into this style at home is a **no-knead crusty
loaf** baked in a lidded Dutch oven, which traps steam for a bakery-like crust with almost
no technique.

*Source: [King Arthur Baking — No-Knead Crusty White Bread](https://www.kingarthurbaking.com/recipes/no-knead-crusty-white-bread-recipe)*

## Best quick option (no yeast, no waiting)

Reach for a **quick bread** or **soda bread**. These skip yeast entirely and rise with
**chemical leaveners** — baking soda (which needs an acid like buttermilk) or baking
powder — that release carbon dioxide the moment they're mixed and heated. No proofing, no
hours of rising: mix, shape, bake. Irish soda bread, banana bread, beer bread, and cornbread
all live here. Ideal when you want bread *today*.

*Source: [King Arthur Baking — How to make bread without yeast](https://www.kingarthurbaking.com/blog/2020/05/25/how-to-make-bread-without-yeast)*

## Best bread for a total beginner

Two easy on-ramps:

- **No-knead crusty white** — almost no skill required; time does the work. Forgiving and
  impressive. (See **making-bread** for the method.)
- **A simple enriched sandwich loaf** — baked in a pan, so shaping is easy and the result
  is a familiar, soft loaf.

Both are hard to mess up and build confidence fast.

## Which flour should I use?

Flour choice is mostly about **protein**, which drives gluten and chew:

- **Bread flour** (higher protein, ~12–13%) — more gluten, more chew and structure. Best
  for lean, chewy, crusty breads and anything you want to rise tall.
- **All-purpose flour** (~11–12%) — versatile; fine for sandwich loaves, soft breads, and
  most everyday baking. Slightly less chew than bread flour.
- **Whole wheat flour** (higher in bran/germ) — nuttier, denser, absorbs more water;
  often blended with white flour for lift. Great for hearty, rustic loaves.

When in doubt for a chewy artisan loaf, use bread flour; for a soft sandwich loaf,
all-purpose is perfectly happy.

*Source: [King Arthur Baking — What does protein percentage mean?](https://www.kingarthurbaking.com/blog/2023/09/25/protein-percentage)*

## Quick reference

| You want… | Make… | Dough family |
|---|---|---|
| Sandwiches / toast | Soft sandwich loaf, milk bread, brioche | Enriched |
| Crusty, chewy, artisan | Baguette, ciabatta, sourdough, no-knead boule | Lean |
| Bread *today*, no yeast | Soda bread, banana/quick bread, beer bread | Chemical leavening |
| An easy first loaf | No-knead crusty white, or a pan sandwich loaf | Either |
| Maximum chew/structure | Anything with **bread flour** | — |
